# ACP Scoring Methodology

This document defines how results of an **Active Cyber Program (ACP) Assessment** are evaluated and how certification decisions are made.

The scoring methodology ensures that assessments are **consistent, transparent, and comparable** across organizations.

---

# Scoring Model

Each control within the ACP framework is evaluated using the following scoring scale:

0 – Not Implemented
The control does not exist or is not implemented.

1 – Partially Implemented
The control is partially implemented or inconsistently applied.

2 – Implemented
The control is implemented and documented.

3 – Actively Managed
The control is actively monitored, reviewed, and continuously improved.

---

# Control Scoring

Each assessment question contributes to the score of its corresponding control.

The control score is calculated as:

Average of all question scores within the control

---

# Domain Scoring

Each domain consists of multiple controls.

The domain score is calculated as:

Average of all control scores within the domain

---

# Overall ACP Score

The overall ACP score is calculated as:

Average of all domain scores

---

# Critical Domains

Certain domains are considered critical for operating an **active cybersecurity program**.

The following domains are defined as critical:

• Business & Security Governance  
• Identity & Access Management  
• Data Protection & Encryption  
• Monitoring & Incident Response  

Organizations must meet minimum requirements in these domains to qualify for certification.

---

# Minimum Certification Requirements

To receive an **ACP Certification**, the following criteria must typically be met:

• Overall ACP Score ≥ 2.0  
• Critical Domains Score ≥ 1.5  
• No critical security gaps identified  

Failure to meet these requirements may result in:

• Certification Pending (improvements required)  
• Certification Not Granted  

---

# Certification Levels

ACP defines the following maturity levels:

Level 1 – Initial  
Level 2 – Managed  
Level 3 – Defined  
Level 4 – Measured  
Level 5 – Optimized  

The maturity level is derived from the overall ACP score.

---

# Maturity Level Mapping

Example mapping:

0.0 – 0.9 → Level 1 – Initial  
1.0 – 1.9 → Level 2 – Managed  
2.0 – 2.4 → Level 3 – Defined  
2.5 – 2.9 → Level 4 – Measured  
3.0 → Level 5 – Optimized  

---

# Handling of Critical Findings

Certain findings may override scoring results.

Examples:

• Absence of basic access controls  
• No backup strategy  
• No incident response capability  

In such cases, certification may be denied regardless of score.

---

# Evidence Requirement

Scores must be supported by **evidence**.

Examples:

• policies and documentation  
• system configurations  
• logs and monitoring data  
• reports and screenshots  

If no evidence is provided, the control should not be scored higher than:

1 – Partially Implemented

---

# Consistency of Assessments

To ensure consistency across assessments:

• standardized questionnaires must be used  
• evidence must be documented  
• scoring must follow defined rules  

---

# Certification Authority

Final certification decisions are made by:

**Wechsler Information Solution**

---

# Disclaimer

The ACP assessment represents an evaluation of cybersecurity practices at a specific point in time.

It does not guarantee the absence of vulnerabilities or security incidents.
