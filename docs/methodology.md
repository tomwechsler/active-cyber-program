# ACP Methodology

This document describes the methodology behind the **Active Cyber Program (ACP)** framework.

The ACP methodology defines how cybersecurity programs are evaluated in a **structured, transparent, and evidence-based manner**.

The goal is to determine whether an organization operates an **active cybersecurity program** that integrates governance, operational processes, and technical controls.

---

# Methodological Approach

The ACP framework evaluates cybersecurity programs using a combination of:

* governance evaluation
* operational process evaluation
* technical control verification
* evidence-based validation
* maturity assessment

This approach ensures that cybersecurity is assessed **holistically across the organization**.

---

## Evidence-Based Validation

ACP assessments rely on **evidence-based validation**.

Organizations must demonstrate that cybersecurity practices are actively implemented through supporting evidence.

Evidence may include:

* security documentation
* operational records
* system configurations
* monitoring reports

# Assessment Outcome

The ACP methodology produces a structured evaluation of the organization's cybersecurity program.

The outcome typically includes:

* domain scores
* overall cybersecurity maturity
* identified strengths
* improvement recommendations

Organizations that meet the certification criteria may receive the **Active Cyber Program (ACP) Certification**.

---

# Methodological Principles

The ACP methodology follows several key methodological principles.

## Structured Evaluation

Cybersecurity is evaluated using clearly defined domains and controls.

This ensures consistency across assessments.

---

## Evidence-Based Assessment

Assessments rely on documented evidence and operational validation rather than theoretical assumptions.

---

## Practical Applicability

The methodology is designed to be practical and applicable across organizations of different sizes and industries.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Organizations are encouraged to use assessment results to strengthen their cybersecurity programs over time.

---

# Continuous Development

The ACP methodology will evolve over time as cybersecurity threats, technologies, and practices change.

Framework updates may incorporate:

* lessons learned from assessments
* new cybersecurity risks
* improvements in security practices
* feedback from organizations and assessors
