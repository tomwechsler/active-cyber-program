# ACP Governance

This document describes the governance structure of the **Active Cyber Program (ACP)** framework.

The purpose of ACP governance is to ensure that the framework is **maintained, developed, and applied consistently**.

---

# Framework Ownership

The **Active Cyber Program (ACP)** framework is owned and maintained by:

**Wechsler Information Solution**

Germany • Switzerland • Austria

Wechsler Information Solution is responsible for:

* maintaining the ACP framework
* publishing framework updates
* defining assessment methodology
* managing the ACP certification program
* maintaining the ACP trust label

---

# Certification Authority

The **ACP Certification** may only be issued by **Wechsler Information Solution**.

Organizations or individuals may use the ACP framework for:

* internal cybersecurity assessments
* cybersecurity improvement initiatives
* educational purposes

However, they may **not issue ACP certifications** or represent themselves as an official ACP certification authority.

Only assessments performed under the authority of **Wechsler Information Solution** may result in an official **Active Cyber Program Certification**.

---

# Authorized Assessors

ACP assessments are conducted by qualified assessors following the ACP methodology.

Assessors may include:

* internal assessors from Wechsler Information Solution
* authorized partners
* qualified cybersecurity professionals working under ACP assessment guidelines

Authorized assessors must follow:

* the ACP assessment process
* the ACP scoring model
* the ACP evidence requirements

---

# Framework Development

The ACP framework is continuously developed and improved.

Framework updates may include:

* improvements to control domains
* updates to the assessment checklist
* updates to certification requirements
* improvements based on practical assessment experience
* updates based on evolving cybersecurity threats

New framework versions may be published periodically.

---

# Versioning

The ACP framework follows a versioning approach.

Example:

ACP Framework 1.0
ACP Framework 1.1
ACP Framework 2.0

Version updates may include:

* minor improvements
* additional controls
* methodological improvements

Organizations may be assessed against a specific framework version.

---

# Transparency

The ACP framework is published to promote transparency and improve cybersecurity practices.

Public availability of the framework helps organizations understand how cybersecurity programs can be structured and evaluated.

Transparency supports:

* trust in the assessment methodology
* consistent evaluation practices
* cybersecurity improvement across organizations

---

# Intellectual Property

The ACP framework documentation is maintained by **Wechsler Information Solution**.

Organizations may reference and apply the framework internally, but the framework name, certification program, and trust label remain part of the ACP program governance.

Unauthorized use of the ACP certification or trust label is not permitted.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Cybersecurity evolves continuously.

The ACP framework will evolve to reflect:

* emerging threats
* new technologies
* lessons learned from assessments
* improvements in cybersecurity practices

Feedback from organizations and assessors may contribute to future framework improvements.
