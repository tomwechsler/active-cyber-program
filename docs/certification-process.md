# ACP Certification Process

This document describes the standard process for obtaining an **Active Cyber Program (ACP) Certification**.

The process ensures a **structured, transparent, and consistent evaluation** of an organization’s cybersecurity program.

---

# Overview

The ACP certification process consists of the following phases:

1. Initial Request
2. Scope Definition
3. Assessment Preparation
4. Assessment Execution
5. Evidence Review
6. Scoring
7. Certification Decision
8. Certification Issuance
9. Certification Registry

---

# 1. Initial Request

The organization expresses interest in an ACP assessment.

Typical activities:

• Initial contact with Wechsler Information Solution  
• High-level discussion of objectives  
• Clarification of expectations  

---

# 2. Scope Definition

The scope of the assessment is defined.

This includes:

• organizational units  
• systems and environments  
• cloud or on-premise scope  

The scope is formally agreed before the assessment begins.

---

# 3. Assessment Preparation

Preparation phase before the assessment.

Typical activities:

• scheduling of interviews  
• identification of key stakeholders  
• request for documentation and evidence  

---

# 4. Assessment Execution

The ACP assessment is conducted.

This may include:

• interviews with IT, security, and management  
• review of policies and documentation  
• technical validation of systems and controls  

---

# 5. Evidence Review

Collected evidence is analyzed.

This includes:

• validation of documentation  
• verification of configurations  
• review of logs and reports  

Only verified evidence is considered for scoring.

---

# 6. Scoring

Each control and domain is evaluated using the ACP scoring methodology.

This includes:

• scoring of individual controls  
• calculation of domain scores  
• calculation of the overall ACP score  

---

# 7. Certification Decision

Based on the results, a certification decision is made.

Possible outcomes:

• ACP Certification Granted  
• ACP Certification Pending (improvements required)  
• ACP Certification Not Granted  

The final decision is made by:

**Wechsler Information Solution**

---

# 8. Certification Issuance

If certification is granted:

• ACP Certificate is issued  
• ACP Trust Label may be used  
• validity period is defined  

---

# 9. Certification Registry

Certified organizations may be listed in the ACP registry.

The registry may include:

• organization name  
• certification level  
• validity period  

---

# Reassessment

Organizations may undergo reassessment:

• periodically (e.g., every 24 months)  
• after significant changes  
• after improvement measures  

---

# Continuous Improvement

ACP encourages organizations to continuously improve their cybersecurity posture.

Follow-up assessments may be conducted to track progress.
