# ACP Principles

The **Active Cyber Program (ACP)** framework is based on a set of core principles that guide how cybersecurity programs should be structured, evaluated, and continuously improved.

These principles define the philosophy behind the ACP framework and support a practical and effective approach to cybersecurity management.

---

# 1. Cybersecurity is a Business Responsibility

Cybersecurity is not only a technical function.

Organizations must integrate cybersecurity into business governance, strategic decision making, and operational management.

Leadership involvement is essential to ensure that cybersecurity supports business objectives and organizational resilience.

---

# 2. Security Must Be Actively Managed

The ACP framework focuses on **active cybersecurity management**.

Security controls alone are not sufficient if they are not monitored, maintained, and improved.

An effective cybersecurity program requires:

* regular review of security practices
* monitoring of security events
* continuous operational oversight

---

# 3. Risk-Driven Security

Cybersecurity should be guided by risk management.

Organizations must identify and evaluate cyber risks and prioritize security measures based on potential impact and likelihood.

Risk-driven security ensures that cybersecurity investments focus on protecting critical assets and operations.

---

# 4. Evidence-Based Assessment

ACP assessments are based on **evidence rather than assumptions**.

Organizations must demonstrate that cybersecurity controls and processes are actively implemented.

Evidence may include:

* documented policies and procedures
* operational records
* system configurations
* monitoring reports

Evidence-based assessment ensures transparency and objectivity.

---

# 5. Security Across the Entire Organization

Cybersecurity must be addressed across all relevant areas of the organization.

This includes:

* governance and leadership
* operational processes
* IT infrastructure
* data protection
* incident response

A fragmented approach to cybersecurity is not sufficient to manage modern cyber threats.

---

# 6. Continuous Improvement

Cybersecurity is not a one-time project.

Threat landscapes evolve constantly, requiring organizations to continuously improve their cybersecurity capabilities.

Continuous improvement includes:

* regular assessments
* security reviews
* vulnerability management
* security awareness training

Organizations operating an **Active Cyber Program** continuously strengthen their security posture.

---

# 7. Practical and Scalable Security

The ACP framework is designed to be practical and scalable.

Organizations of different sizes and industries should be able to apply the framework.

The goal is not to create unnecessary complexity, but to enable organizations to establish **effective and sustainable cybersecurity programs**.

---

# Conclusion

The ACP principles form the foundation of the **Active Cyber Program framework**.

They guide organizations in developing cybersecurity programs that are:

* structured
* actively managed
* risk-driven
* continuously improved

These principles support the goal of establishing resilient and trustworthy cybersecurity practices across organizations.
